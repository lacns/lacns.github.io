bio: I am a research Master’s student in neuroscience at Vrije University Amsterdam. I am currently completing my final-year research project in the Psychology of Language department at the MPI for Psycholinguistics and the Poeppel Lab at the Ernst Strüngmann Institute for Neuroscience. In this project, we assess the role of syllabic units for setting sensory goals in the planning and monitoring of speech. We exploit the syllable-frequency effect (viz., high-frequency syllables are produced faster than low-frequency syllables) and analyse EEG signals during phonetic encoding and auditory self-suppression. The first part of my PhD project will focus on similar issues arising at the intersection between Psycholinguistics and speech motor control. I will work under the supervision of Andrea Martin and Antje Meyer, and in collaboration with David Poeppel and members of his team.
